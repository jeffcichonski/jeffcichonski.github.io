Our Story

Christina (The Bride) Andrew (The Groom)
Born September 2, 1988. Born August 9th, 1990.

It all started in the summer of 2006 at Wegmans, where Christina and Andrew both worked. Christina was known as “Boss Lady” and remains ever since. They spent many months as friends until July 5th, 2007 when they started officially dating. Christina left for college shortly after at West Chester University. They spent many months driving back and forth to spend time together. Eventually, Christina transferred to a school closer to home, and rejoined Andrew at Wegmans, working together again.

In 2010, Christina and Andrew got their dog, Bentley, and he has been their little troublemaker ever since. Later that year, they moved into an apartment together in Fort Lauderdale, Fl.

On December 13, 2013, Andrew took Christina to Jimmy's Restaurant and Chocolate Shop for dinner, a place they frequent. He surprised her with a table on the balcony, champagne and chocolate covered strawberries, and asked her to marry him. She of course said yes!

Now, Andrew, Christina and Bentley are living in Northern Fort Lauderdale, planning their wedding. They feel like they are already living the married life, yet can’t wait to make it official on April 18th, 2015! They hope you can be there to celebrate with them!

    Andrew Barson

    Groomsman

    Andy and I first met before we entered the sixth grade at Springhouse Middle School. I can remember thinking that Andy is the coolest kid on the block. It seemed as if he had everything going for him. Merely by the age of 10, Andy had already acquired his very own cell phone, girlfriend, and not only one, but two Go-Ped motorized scooters. I thought to myself, “this is a guy after my own heart.” Of course, we instantly hit it off. Andy and I shared great memories throughout the years that followed. Together, along with a close group of friends, we outgrew our adolescence and matured into adulthood. As our time at Parkland High School began to wind down, Andy got a part time job at Wegmans, the local supermarket. For some odd reason, Andy was extremely eager to push shopping carts every chance he could get, even while it was extremely cold outside. Not even a blizzard would keep him from pushing those carts. When I would visit Andy at work, I never understood how he could possibly enjoy pushing shopping carts so much on the coldest days of winter. It was like he was on a mission and nothing could get in his way. Upon one visit however, I was introduced to his new boss, Christina. At that moment, everything finally made sense. It was obvious that Christina had to be extremely special for Andy to trench through that slush filled parking lot, day and night, just so he could spend more time with her. After a few winters like that, I was not surprised when Andy said he would be moving to Florida. In the end, it looks like all those freezing nights definitely paid off. I am very proud to call Andy one of my best friends and extremely happy to see him journey through life with Christina.
